Storage

So, now its time to probably make one of the easiest decisions, but, that doesn't mean you shouldn't put at least a little thought into it, if you don't have enough storage, you'll find yourself deleting stuff all day, if you have too much, well then that's not a problem at all which is why I actually recommend getting 50-100 GB more than what you need to future proof because apps, as well as the OS, is always getting larger so having extra space is going to be very nice for the future.


But, how much should you get? Well that question really depends on you, are you planning on doing gaming, well you probably will want to start with 1TB but if you use lots of apps or store a lot of large files then you will also need a lot but it really just depends on you, here are some questions to help you choose. How much do you currently have? Is your current storage enough for you? If you don't have enough storage, how much would be enough to make you comfortable?


Ok, so now you have a number but what storage device should you go with, well there are three main types of storage, there are Hard Drives (HDD), there are solid-state drives (SSD), and lastly, there is M.2.


HDD

HDD is a short form for Hard Disk Drive, the Hard Drive is the slowest in this list as it has been around for many years, but the hard drive is still great for PC’s today however they will affect load time significantly, hard drives are also known for breaking easier, especially if you drop it, so if you are getting a laptop then make sure you don’t have a hard drive in it. I also don’t recommend a hard drive if you are trying to make a fast PC as if you put a Hard Drive in it, even if you have really powerful parts the hard drive is going to really affect performance.


SSD

Next, we have the SSD, what does SSD stand for? SSD stands for Solid State Drive and is a huge update over the standard Hard Drive, this is the part I recommend for anyone who doesn’t need ultimate speed for things like gaming or professional video editing, the SSD is very fast and also way more reliable than the SSD and can almost not be broken by accident so if you need a PC then I really recommend the SSD as it is only beaten by the M.2 SSD which you should also read about before choosing.


M.2

Finally, on this list we have the M.2 SSD, why talk about it separately, well the M.2 SSD is significantly different from the normal SSD from size to form factor to where it connects. First off let's talk about the form factor, M.2 SSD’s are one of the smallest forms of storage devices for computers, they are also very fast which means that they are going to need their own proprietary slot whereas the standard SSD just uses the same SATA connection as the Hard Drive.

M.2 SSD’s also have multiple standards and these standards are just to represent speed and if you want the fastest then you will want to get an m.2 SSD with the NVMe Standard as those can get up to a couple of GB/s.


Choosing

When it comes to choosing the correct type here is a little guidance, I don't really recommend a Hard Drive at all as the main storage device although you could use it as a secondary device, the SSD is great for the average user, and I would recommend the M.2 NVMe SSD if you need very fast speeds for things like vide3o editing or if you do this that require writing large file a lot, I also recommend it for gaming as it can give you the fastest load times and best performance although other parts such as CPU and GPU also matter a lot.
