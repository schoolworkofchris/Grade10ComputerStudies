Power Supply

Finally, it’s time to get our power supply. What is the power supply? The power supply is in charge of delivering power to every component of your computer and today I am going to help you choose the power supply for you.


Wattage

When hunting for a power supply, you will notice that they all have a wattage written on it, this number is very important, it is ok for the number to be higher then needed as it will just automatically adjust itself to the right voltage needed, but if the number is too low then you can't do anything about it, all you can do is get a different power supply which is why it is very important to calculate the amount of power each part needs then get something that is around 50 - 100 watts more then you needed just to be safe.


Types

There are also different types of power supplies, there are modular. Semi-modular and non-modular. Below is and explanations of each.


Semi-modular

A semi-modular power supply is a power supply that has some cables that can get disconnected, only the necessary cables are connected to it, this is very nice for cable management as you won’t be left with a ton of unused cable around.


Modular

A modular power supply is almost like a semi-modular power supply but on a modular power supply, you can disconnect every single cable from the power supply.


Non-modular

And last, we have the non-modular power supply, a non-modular power supply has all the cable hard connected to it and cannot be taken off unless cut off, this is the most unorganized power supply as it will leave you with a ton of extra cables that you will just have to shove away to the side in your case.


Conclusion

If you are looking for a power supply that will keep your PC organized go for the semi-modular or the modular power supply and if you want to save some money and don’t care about organization then just go for the non-modular power supply.
